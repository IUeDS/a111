In the bottom right corner of the full schematic shown in Figure 2-1 you will see the power supply. Figure 3-1 focuses on this section.

The components in this section are (in order from AC input to DC output:

  • AC switch
    • Purpose: allow user to turn unit on or off.

  • Fuse
    • Purpose: prevent damage by stopping current flow if maximum is exceeded.

  • 0.047uF capacitor
    • Purpose: remove noise from AC power line.

    • How? A capacitor in parallel creates a low pass filter. This allows the AC power line 60Hz (or 50Hz) to pass but cuts higher frequencies, where noise is contained.

  • TR1 transformer
    • Purpose: Converts power line voltage (~120$V$ in U.S.) to voltages needed by circuit. In this case the secondary voltages are:

      • $320V_{RMS}$: Supplies large voltage to anodes.

      • $5V_{RMS}$: Supplies heater voltage to full wave rectifier

      • $6.3V_{RMS}$: Supplies heater voltage to other tubes

  • 5y3GT full wave rectifier tube:
    • Purpose = convert AC into DC

    • Coverts $320V_{RMS}$ to $+360V_{DC}$

  • 20µF capacitors (all three)
    • Purpose = "flatten" rectified AC into DC

    • How? A capacitor in parallel creates a low pass filter. The varying portion of the voltage (AC) is sent through the capacitor with a low reactance. This leaves only flat DC for the circuit.

  • 1kΩ and 10kΩ resistors
    • Purpose = Reduce voltage for preamplifier and driver sections.

    • How? A resistor in series creates a voltage drop.
