Whether or not you have taken an online course before, this course may be set up differently.
Please contact me any time you have questions. 

Wait! How do I contact you? To contact me you can:

  • send a message through the Canvas Inbox
  • call my office (812) 856-4897
  • ask a question through the Canvas Discussion tool. (this is particulalry good if the answer could be useful to other students)
  • schedule an appointment for office hours if you are on the Bloomington campus
  • schedule an internet chat

Here are some of the things that you would have in the on-campus courses you are likely used to with a description of how they will be experience in this course:

  • Lectures: I am not a fan of standing in a front of a class and talking as a way to get information to students, I try not to do it too much even in my on-campus courses. The Modules in this course will provide you with reading and video demonstrations, possibly even me talking a bit, of the information you need for each unit of the course. There will likely be short videos added to the course as Announcements each week to clarify points that the class seems to be having trouble getting. The regular reading assessments and games will help me identify where there are issues. Hopefully I will get feedback from students as well such as "I don't get this, help!". Please contact me any time you do not understand part of the course material.
  • Textbook: The Modules contain all the reading that would normally be in a textbook, and it's free!
  • Labs: You will be getting a Lab Kit, provided to you for the semester. With this you will build circuits with actual electronic components for each topic in the course. This is breifcase sized and will only require an electrical outlet and (for some labs) something that plays audio through an 1/8" headphone jack (phone, computer, MP3 player, etc) and a little bit of desk or table space.
  • Face to Face Instructor Time: This is one that is hard to replace and is really up to each student. You will see me in the regular videos but for interactive communication you need to make the effort and contact me. 
  • Homework: Since you can do the entire course from home (and your bathrobe if you wish, though not actually in the bath....) "homework" is a meaningless term.
    There will be questions to be answered and work to be done though. Reading assessments and games are placed within the Modules and will allow me to see whether or not the students are understanding the material. There will also be assignments utilizing Circuit Wizard simulation software and the Lab Kit.
