When you connect electronic components together you have a circuit. We discussed the two main ways to connect circuits in the first unit, they are series (Figure 4-1) and parallel (Figure 4-2).

A series connection (shown in Figure 4-1) occurs when the current follows a single path through all of the components. Components that are in series will all have the same amount of current flow through each component.

When the equivalent leads of two or more components are connected together (as shown in Figure 4-2) they are said to be in parallel. Parallel components have equal voltage drops for each parallel branch.

Most circuits will have both series and parallel sections.

When resistors are connected in series (as shown in Figure 4-1) their values add to make the total resistance of the circuit. This value will always be larger than the largest resistor in the circuit. To find the total resistance of resistors connected in series you simply add their values. $ R_{total} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + ..... $

TIP: The most common mistake when adding resistors in parallel is that students add the inverse of the resistances and then take that as the total resistance. You must invert that to get the actual total.

When resistors are connected in parallel (as in Figure 4-2), however, they actually reduce the circuit resistance. The total resistance will be less than the smallest resistor in the circuit.

Use this formula, called the inverse rule, to add resistors in parallel. $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {1}{R_1} + \frac {1}{R_2} + \frac {1}{R_3} ..... $

If you have a resistor and you add a resistor in parallel with it, the total resistance will always decrease and be less than the smallest parallel resistance.

Total Resistance Solution
Step 1: Choose the correct formula. $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {1}{R_1} + \frac {1}{R_2} + \frac {1}{R_3} + \frac {1}{R_4} $
Step 2: Enter known values. $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {1}{2000\Omega} + \frac {1}{4000\Omega} + \frac {1}{1000\Omega} + \frac {1}{4000\Omega} $
Step 3: Find the least common denominator and make all denominators match that. $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {2}{4000} + \frac {1}{4000} + \frac {4}{4000} + \frac {1}{4000} $
Step 4: Add the fractions. $ \frac {1}{R_{total}} = \frac {8}{4000} $
Step 5: Invert both sides. $ {R_{total}} = \frac {4000}{8} $
Step 6: Solve it! $ {R_{total}} = 500\Omega $
