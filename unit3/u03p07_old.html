If we connect an inductor in series with the load and an inductor in parallel with the load (as shown in figure 9-1) we can combine the high pass filter and low pass filter.

As audio flows through the series inductor, L1, the high frequencies are dropped by the inductor. That means they do not get to the rest of the circuit. After that L2 reduces the low frequencies because it has a lower voltage drop for them and is in parallel with the load.

L1 cuts the highs from our signal and L2 cuts the lows. That leaves us with the frequency response shown in figure 9-2. This is called a band pass filter as it leaves a band to pass while cutting what is above and below.

Note that no audio frequencies get to the load without being cut at least some.
