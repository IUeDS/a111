Capacitors affect the phase of voltage compared to current of signals that pass through them. As with reactance they are the inverse of inductors. With capacitors voltage is delayed so that it changes after current. As with inductors this delay varies with frequency (which is not desirable). Figure 11-1 shows the phase relationships of voltage and current for resistors, inductors, and capacitors.

What It Means

For the purposes of audio circuits, it is important to understand that inductors and capacitors affect the phase of voltage and current in this way. The amount of lag (or delay) between voltage and current will vary with frequency. This can degrade the audio quality; though how much may be up for debate.

One of the solutions to this problem is to use inductors and capacitors together in circuits so that they offset each other. When inductors and capacitors are used together, the different frequencies of audio are more aligned that they would have been with just capacitors. Inductors are much more expensive than capacitors, making capacitors much more common. There are many audio circuits that only use capacitors and sound great. The point of discussing this is to give you the background information and let you then decide for yourself if the added cost of inductors is worthwhile.
