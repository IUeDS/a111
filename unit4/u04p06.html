Capacitors act inversely to inductors which makes their filters also the inverse when used in series and parallel. A series capacitor, as shown in Figure 6-1, creates a high pass filter. The high frequencies are passed more easily because they experience a lower reactance than lower frequencies. Since the lower frequencies experience a higher reactance, more of their voltage is dropped by the capacitor, leaving less to get to the load.

When Capacitance Changes:
Capacitance value increased = curve moves to the left.
Capacitance value decreased = curve moves to the right.

Our rule for changing inductor values works the same for capacitors. As capacitance is increased, the curve moves to the left and as capacitance is decreased the curve moves to the right.
