As with the series capacitor filters, a parallel capacitor creates the inverse filter of an inductor. A parallel capacitor, as shown in Figure 7-1, creates a low pass filter. Because the capacitor is in parallel with the load, the voltage drop of the load and the voltage drop of the capacitor are equal. The lower reactance of the capacitor at high frequencies creates a low voltage drop which means a low voltage across the load. At lower frequencies the higher reactance creates a higher voltage drop across both the capacitor and the load.

Again our rule for changing capacitance remains the same as shown in Figures 7-2 and 7-3.

When Capacitance Changes
Capacitance value increased = curve moves to the left.
Capacitance value decreased = curve moves to the right.
