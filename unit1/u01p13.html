To measure the amount of current in a circuit we have to be able to count how many electrons move past a point in a certain amount of time. You won’t actually be counting the electrons but a current meter can determine the amount of current for you. To do so, the current meter has to be connected to the circuit in such a way that all of the electrons (in the part of the circuit where you want to measure current flow) go through the current meter. If we want to measure current through a light bulb in our circuit (two light bulbs in series) how would we connect the meter?

Remember we have two basic ways to connect components: series and parallel. We want every electron that is flowing through the light bulb (that we wish to test) to go through the meter. That means we need to connect the meter in series with the light bulb. There are two light bulbs in the circuit shown in Figure 13-1, does it matter which one we connect the current meter in series with? No, because all of the components in this circuit are in series with each other. Once the meter is in series with one light bulb, it is in series with all of them. Since current is the same through the entire series circuit, it does not even matter where we connect the meter. We will want to make sure that the positive side of the meter is connected to the more positive side of the circuit. If we don’t we will get a negative instead of positive number for current.
