The key to understanding electronics, even the most complicated circuits, lies in understanding the charges and forces of electrons. Electricity is, quite simply, electrons moving. So where do they move and why?

We will represent electrons throughout the text with this Blue smiley face.
(why Blue?, see text)

An electron is a negatively charged particle. The most basic tenet of electronics is that like-charged particles repel and oppositely-charged particles attract. For example, if you set 2 magnets on a table with the same poles facing each other, they will move apart; if the opposite poles face each other, they will move together. Electrons, being negatively charged, are attracted by positive (opposite) charges and repelled by negative (like) charges. This is our first basic concept of electronics. Electrons will always flow from a more negative charge to a more positive charge.

First Basic Concept of Electronics
Electrons will ALWAYS flow from a more negative charge to a more positive charge.

In electronics the charge that moves the electrons is called voltage. Voltage (volts) is the force that moves electrons. There can be positive and negative voltages but there can also be no charge, which we will call 0 (zero) volts. A positive voltage is typically represented by the color red. Negative voltages are typically represented as black or blue, while zero volts is represented by the color green.
