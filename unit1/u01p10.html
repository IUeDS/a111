Voltage in Series Circuits

The voltages dropped by components connected in series will add up to the total voltage of the circuit. In the circuit in Figure 10-1 the two light bulbs will divide up the voltage from the 9V battery. Since the light bulbs are the same they will each drop half of the voltage from the battery.

$ \frac {9V} {2} = {4.5V} $ is dropped by each light bulb.

Voltage in Parallel Circuits

In a parallel circuit each branch of the circuit drops the entire voltage applied by the source. In the circuit in Figure 10-2, that means that each light bulb will get the entire 9V.

If you think about our door analogy from 1-4 this makes sense. The entire voltage applied to the parallel circuit would be applied to both of the light bulbs equally. This is different from the series circuit where the voltage is applied across the combination of the two light bulbs so it is divided up between the two.
