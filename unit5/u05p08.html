Tube Numbering

Tubes are given a letter/number combination to indicate the type of tube. Figure 8-1 shows the US standard. The 12AX7 is probably the most commonly used tube today.

  • Heater Voltage: It uses 12.6V for heaters, though it can use 6.3 when the two heaters are connected in parallel instead of series.

  • Sequence Code: The sequence code tells us that this is a high-mu dual triode.

    • There is no particular order to the sequence code, they were simply added as needed.

  • Number of Elements: It has 2 triodes in one vacuum case.

    • That makes 6 elements plus the heater for 7 total elements.

Tube Specifications

Figure 8-2 shows the data-sheet detailing all the information you would need to know about the 12AX7. This tells you what pin numbers on the tube correspond to what connections within the tube. You will need this information when working with the tubes. This is included as an example simply of what the data-sheet might look like. Some of the important points you would look for are heater voltage and current. Many data-sheets (though not this one) would also show maximum voltage and current for the anode.

You can find a datasheet like this for any vacuum tube you would want to use.
