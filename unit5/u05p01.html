The first vacuum tube was created by Sir John Ambrose Fleming in 1904, he called it the oscillation valve. It later became known as a diode because it had two elements. The two elements, or essential parts, were the anode and cathode.

The diode was based on observations by one of Edison’s scientists while working on improving the light bulb in 1883. One of the keys to the light bulb lasting longer was placing it inside a vacuum. While trying to make them last even longer one of Edison’s scientists (William Hammer) placed an additional element inside the "vacuum" of the bulb. He noted that there was a current flowing into it. They called this the "Edison Effect". This didn’t help the longevity of the bulb so he only noted the effect without pursuing it.

Edison later said that he wasn’t sure why they didn’t follow it up, but that he didn’t even understand Ohm’s law (1827) at that point. The electron was still 15 years from being discovered.
