We know that the anode must be more positive and the cathode more negative. What would happen if AC was connected to a diode?

  • Diodes allow current flow in only one direction so only half of the AC waveform would be able to pass through the diode.

Think of it as a closed switch for the positive portion of the wave and an open switch for the negative portion of the wave. This is called rectification and is used to convert AC into DC.

Used in this way, the diode is called a half wave rectifier because only half of the wave is converted to DC. Figure 3-1 shows the resulting waveform.

Most diodes are directly heated, as shown in the half wave rectifier circuit schematic in Figure 3-2. With a direct heated half wave rectifier circuit there are 3 connections to the cathode:

  1. One side of the cathode is connected to one side of a 5VAC supply.

  2. The other end of the cathode is connected to the second side of the 5VAC supply.

  3. One of the two ends of the cathode (it does not matter which) is connected as the output.

We now have a component which will only allow current to flow in one direction. We can use this to convert AC into DC.
